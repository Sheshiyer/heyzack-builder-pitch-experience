### Language Rules (DO Use)

**B2B Context:**
- ✅ ROI specificity: "Cut operating costs by 28% in Q1"
- ✅ Operational reality: "No $3K service calls for thermostat changes"
- ✅ Peer credibility: "Here's what actually works..."
- ✅ Problem acknowledgment: "You've been burned by BMS vendors"

**B2C Context:**
- ✅ Benefit clarity: "Save $47/month on average"
- ✅ Relatable problems: "Tired of seven different apps?"
- ✅ Honest simplicity: "Set up in 30 minutes"
- ✅ Validation: "You're not crazy—smart homes *have* been frustrating"

### Language Rules (DON'T Use)

**Avoid These Patterns:**
- ❌ Vague fluff: "Revolutionary platform" → ✅ "One dashboard for all properties"
- ❌ Jargon without context: "Leverage IoT architecture" → ✅ "WiFi, Zigbee, Matter support"
- ❌ Hype: "Transform your lifestyle" → ✅ "Save time and money daily"
- ❌ Guilt/shame: "Still using outdated systems?" → ✅ "Ready to upgrade?"

- "Fast. Powerful. Easy to use."
- "The best camera ever in an iPhone."
- "All-day battery life. And then some."

**Structure:** State benefit. Explain how. Technical foundation (small text).

### Active Voice, Present Tense

❌ Passive/Future: "Energy can be saved through automated scheduling"  
✅ Active/Present: "It learns when you're home. Adjusts automatically."

## TONE CHECKLIST (Before Publishing Hero Pages)

### B2B Checklist
- [ ] Contains specific ROI data or savings percentages
- [ ] Addresses vendor dependency or operational pain point
- [ ] Uses "you" language (direct address)
- [ ] Mentions portfolio/multi-property scale
- [ ] Avoids consumer-focused emotional language
- [ ] Includes proof point (case study, data, spec)
- [ ] Sounds like peer advice, not vendor pitch
- [ ] Respects reader's intelligence and time

### B2C Checklist
- [ ] Uses "you" language (direct address)
- [ ] Avoids technical jargon or explains it immediately
- [ ] Contains specific benefit or outcome (not vague promise)
- [ ] Acknowledges common frustration or pain point
- [ ] Sounds like a friend explaining, not a company pitching
- [ ] Includes proof point (savings amount, setup time)
- [ ] Respects intelligence without assuming expertise
- [ ] Passes "non-tech partner test" (would they understand?)

## WRITING WORKFLOW FOR 15 HERO PAGES

**Step 1: Extract Product Data**
- Pull specs from product_catalog.json
- Note existing description (reference point, not copy-paste)
- Identify unique features vs. other products in category

**Step 2: Identify 3-4 Key Benefits**
- What problem does this solve?
- What's the "aha!" moment for users?
- What differentiates it from competitors?
- What enables builder/property use cases?

**Step 3: Write Apple-Style Headline**
- Short sentence or fragment
- Benefit-focused (what it does for you)
- Active language
- Examples: "See who's there. Before they knock." | "Every room. Perfect temperature."

**Step 4: Write Benefit Sections**
- Lead with benefit headline (3-5 words)
- 2-3 sentences explaining benefit
- "Technical Foundation" callout (small text, specs)
- Alternate benefit order across pages (keep fresh)

**Step 5: Add B2B Section**
- "For Builders & Developers" headline
- Installation at scale benefits
- Portfolio management benefits
- Tenant satisfaction benefits
- Bulk pricing mention

**Step 6: Peer Review**
- Does it sound like Apple wrote it?
- Is copy unique (not repeated from other pages)?
- Does it serve both B2B and B2C?
- Are specs accurate per product_catalog.json?
- Passes tone checklists above?
