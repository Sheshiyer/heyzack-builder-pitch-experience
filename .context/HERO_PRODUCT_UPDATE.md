## APPLE WRITING PRINCIPLES APPLIED

### Core Patterns Extracted

1. **Lead with Benefit, Not Spec**
   - ❌ "2K resolution sensor with H.265 compression"
   - ✅ "See faces in vivid detail. Day or night."

2. **Short Sentences. Declarative.**
   - Apple: "Fast. Powerful. Easy to use."
   - HeyZack: "Smart. Secure. Saves money."

3. **Active Voice, Present Tense**
   - ❌ "Energy can be saved through automation"
   - ✅ "It learns when you're home. Adjusts automatically."

4. **Feature → Benefit Pairing**
   - State benefit headline
   - Explain in 2-3 sentences
   - Add "Technical Foundation" callout (small text)

5. **Avoid Marketing Fluff**
   - ❌ "Revolutionary next-generation innovation"
   - ✅ "Works. Reliably. For years."

6. **Use Concrete Numbers**
   - ❌ "Significant energy savings"
   - ✅ "28% lower utility bills on average"

7. **Sensory, Visceral Language**
   - ❌ "Provides notification alerts"
   - ✅ "Your phone vibrates the moment someone's at the door"

## QUALITY STANDARDS

### Tone Checklist (Both B2B & B2C)
- [ ] Uses "you" language (direct address)
- [ ] Avoids jargon or explains it immediately
- [ ] Contains specific benefit/outcome (not vague)
- [ ] Acknowledges common pain point
- [ ] Sounds like peer/friend, not vendor pitch
- [ ] Includes proof point (savings, setup time, metric)
- [ ] Respects intelligence without assuming expertise

### Uniqueness Check
- [ ] Copy is unique to this product (not repeated)
- [ ] Benefits emphasize what makes THIS product special
- [ ] B2B angle tailored to specific builder use case
- [ ] No generic "smart home automation" language
